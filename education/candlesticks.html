Candlestick Patterns

Understanding price action without indicators

What Is a Candlestick?

A candlestick shows four things: Open, High, Low, and Close. It tells you who controlled the market — buyers or sellers.

Important Candlestick Patterns

As a beginner, focus on a few strong patterns. More patterns = more confusion.

1. Bullish Engulfing

A large bullish candle completely engulfs the previous bearish candle. It often signals buyers taking control.

2. Bearish Engulfing

A strong bearish candle engulfs a bullish candle. It often signals selling pressure.

3. Pin Bar (Rejection Candle)

A candle with a long wick and small body. Shows strong rejection from a price level.

Common Beginner Mistake

Candlestick patterns are NOT entry signals alone. Always combine them with: levels, trend, or sessions.
